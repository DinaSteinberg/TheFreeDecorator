Your Kitchen Is For Cooking!

Organizing you kitchen to make cooking easier.

It's late at night and you're still up trying to finish that last little thing. After your third
yawn in a row, you decide you need a little sugar boost to keep you going just a bit more. You amble
over to the fridge and grope around for something sugary. And then you stop. Something slimy and
gloopy covers your hand, and you hurry to wipe the repulsive slime from your hand. Well thatdefinitely
woke you up.

If you can relate to the story above, you're definitely not alone. But why shuold it stay like this?
There are so many available resources to help you easily organize your kitchen! Below, you'll find
some of these helpful items that once you get, you'll wonder how you managed without.

The thin pull-out spice cabinet

As you install your cabinets, if you find that some cabinets are just too wide, make it just 5 inches
thinner and add this handy dandy spice organizer. It's convenient, and saves you the overwhelming task
of trying to take out one spice without having them all fall on your head. This is a good alternative
to swivel spice cabinets because, A) You can see all your spices at once with this pull-out cabinet,
and B)It takes up much less room, room that's not used anyways.

Corner cabinets

You know those annoying corner cabinets that you have to crawl into to get anything from? Well this will
change all of that. With these corner cabinet racks, all you need to do is pull out the rack, and it
all comes out to greet you. Instead of having wasted cabinet space, you can utilize it with this amazing
innovation. This is an incredible investment, and you'll be thanking yourself for getting it for years to
come.

Containers!

Are you sick of having to deal with all the boxes and bags and oddly shaped containers the food you buy
comes in? This might be an obvious one for some of you, but if it isn't, go out and buy some airtight
containers! They're life changers. But warning: make sure to wash them in between uses. They last very
well, but you don't want mold growing in your cereal or whatever else is in your container. In the picture
above, the containers have labels. Not neccessary, but can be helpful.

You have to be choosy about what you store in these containers. Don't feel pressured to store everything in
containers. If you have big snack bags that are open, just use bag clips to close them; you don't need to put
them into a container. You'll have no more room in your pantry!

Lid Holder

Tupperware containers are great and useful. They're good for lunches, storing leftovers, keeping cut
veggies... but there's a major downside to these containers: storing them. They come in all different
shapes and sizes, and storing them and their covers is a nightmare. So this is where the lid organizer
comes in. It's here to help you deal with the mess and have some organization in your cabinets. Stick this
into your cabinet and admire the neat stack of cover-less containers. And the last minute rush to find
the right cover? Gone.

Pantry Lazy Susan

While Lazy Susans are meant for food serving, they come in handy in cabinets and pantries. Many people
use 2 or 3 tiered Lazy Susans to store spices, but you can store other items on them too. These come in
handy in pantries; instead of having to rifle through everything , you can just twirl the turntable
and easily get what you need. Major time saver, and anguish saver too!
