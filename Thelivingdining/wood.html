Walnut

Walnut grows from Vermont to the Great Plains and south through Louisiana
and Texas. Its color varies from white to dark brown, and it's known
for its large burls. Famously hardy, walnut was used to make airplane
propellers in World War I.

Maple

Maple is found primarily in the Great Lakes region. Its color ranges from
cream to a red-brown and it has a straight grain. Many scholars believe
the frame of the Trojan horse was constructed with maple.

Mahogony

Mahogany is naive to Central and South America, but it's grown throughout
the tropics. Its color varies from a plae pink to a red-brown with a generally
straight grain. It's often usedto build instuments, giving the sound a warm tone.

Birch

Birch grows in the Northeast, Great Lakes and Appalachian regions. Its
color ranges from cream to a light red-brown with a fine grain. Howard
Hughes' famous H-4 Hercules aircraft was made of birch.

Oak

Most oak comes from the eastern and central U.S. The Color ranges from
light brown to pink-red with a swirling or striped grain. The current Oval
Office desk is made of oak and was a gift from Queen Victoria.

Cherry

Cherry is found in the eastern U.S. Its distinctive color starts as a
pink-brown and darkens to a deep red, with a straight grain and some rippling.
Contrary to popular belief, George Washington never confessed to cutting
down a cherry tree.

Bamboo

Bamboo is known for its eco-friendly nature. Its color varies from light to
red-brown, but naturally, it boasts a blonde color. Bamboo stalks are
actually a type of grass, which allows for frequent harvesting without
replanting.

Pine

Pine is found throughout the Northern Hemisphere. Its color ranges from
white to light brown with yellow or red tones, and it's known for its
beautiful knots. The oldest living pine tree in the world is a bristlecone
pine that's 5,065 years old.
